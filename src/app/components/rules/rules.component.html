Game Setup

1. Board Setup

  • Standard map size is 120cm x 80cm
  • Define grid sectors based on the board/table dimensions.
  • Each sector is 10cm x 10cm
  • Grid columns are defined by numbers, while rows are defined by letters.
  • Thus a sector will be defined by a word letter combination. E.g G4
  • Three initial areas are defined based on the sectors.
    • 1/4 Player A Area
    • 2/4 Neutral Zone
    • 1/4 Player B Area

2. Terrain Setup

  • Players each place a control point within their initial play area.
  • There are two kinds of structures. Props and control points.
  • Players take turns placing props.
  • Players take turns placing control points in the neutral area.
  • A sector may not contain two structures at the same time.
  • A structure may not be placed in a sector adjacent to a sector that already contains a structure.

Turn Phases

1. Upkeep Phase

  • The player receives resoures from all their control points.

2. Deployment Phase

  • The player may spend their available resources to deploy units from their reserve within sectors that contain control points under their command.

3. Artillery Phase

  • The player may use their artillery to define bombardment zones that are within range of their deployed artillery pieces.
  • Units within a bombardment zone have the options to attempt to escape the bombardment.
  • Units which have used movement within this phase cannot move during the next action phase.
  • The player rolls to attack with each artillery piece and hits are immediately assigned.

4. Action Phase

  • Each unit may perform either
    • One Movement and One Action
    • Two Movements
    • Two Actions

4.1 Movement

  • The player may move each of their units up to the maximum movement value described on their unit card.
  • Units may move twice their movement value, but they will not be able to attack on the same round.

4.2 Actions

  • The player may choose to execute an action command for any of their units.
  • A unit may execute only one action each round, unless specified otherwise.
  • Both attacks and special abilities are considered actions.
  • The attacking units roll combat for each miniature.
  • The defending units roll combat for each miniature if possible.
  • Mark down each hit for each unit.
  • Hits are assigned once both units have executed their attacks.
